Moonshot AI Launches Kimi K3 — A 2.8 Trillion Parameter Open-Weight Model

AP News Model Release China AI

Chinese startup Moonshot AI has taken the industry by surprise with Kimi K3, a massive 2.8-trillion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model boasting a 1-million-token context window. Released via API on July 16, the model scores 93.5% on GPQA Diamond, ranking competitively with Claude Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5 on general intelligence while leading on several coding and agentic benchmarks — including #1 on Frontend Code Arena at launch.

  • Architecture: ~2.8T param MoE with always-on reasoning and native vision
  • Context: 1,048,576 tokens (1M) — flat-rate API pricing
  • Open Weights: Full weights expected by July 27 under Modified MIT license
  • Pricing: $3/MTok input, $15/MTok output, $0.30/MTok cached
  • Variants: K3 Max (chat/single-agent) and K3 Swarm Max (multi-agent batch workloads)

Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab Releases First Open Model: Inkling

TechCrunch Model Release Open Source

Thinking Machines Lab, the AI startup founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, has released Inkling — its first open-weights model after 18 months of behind-the-scenes development. Inkling is a multimodal model (text, image, audio in; text out) released under Apache 2.0, designed as a flexible, fine-tuneable alternative to monolithic frontier models. The company raised over $1B in initial funding and positions itself against the "one-size-fits-all" approach of competitors like OpenAI and Anthropic.

  • License: Apache 2.0 — full open weights available immediately
  • Philosophy: Customizable, specialized models over ever-larger general-purpose systems
  • Benchmarks: Competitive on reasoning and coding tasks vs. other open models in its class
  • Significance: First public proof point from one of the most anticipated AI startups

Microsoft & AMD Expand Partnership: Helios Rackscale Deployed on Azure

AMD Newsroom Infrastructure Cloud AI

AMD and Microsoft announced a major expansion of their strategic partnership today. Microsoft will deploy AMD's Helios Rackscale Solution — combining Instinct MI455X GPUs, EPYC "Venice" CPUs, Pensando DPUs, and ROCm software — to power frontier model AI inference on Azure. The collaboration also adds two new Azure VM series (HDv2 for agentic AI, HXv2 for semiconductor design) powered by 6th Gen AMD EPYC processors and deepens integration of AMD Pensando DPUs with Azure Boost for networking.

  • Helios: Open, integrated rackscale platform shipping H2 2026
  • Workloads: Frontier model inference, Azure AI Foundry, enterprise AI
  • Quote from Lisa Su: "Extending partnership across the full stack of AMD AI solutions on Azure"
  • Quote from Satya Nadella: "Expanding Azure infrastructure portfolio with AMD Helios for performance, scale and choice"

Why AI May Never Reach Human Intelligence — The Tacit Knowledge Problem

SciTechDaily / Taylor & Francis AGI Philosophy

Computer scientist Peter J. Denning argues in a new paper, "Turing's Mistake," that AI systems cannot achieve human-level intelligence because they cannot acquire tacit knowledge — the unarticulated understanding behind common sense, intuition, practical skill, and cultural context. He points to the 40-year Cyc project, which after 25 million entries still couldn't give expert systems sufficient common sense. Denning warns that this limitation makes advanced AI both fundamentally different from humans and potentially dangerous to align.

  • Five forms of tacit knowledge elude ML: common sense, everyday interactions, feelings/perceptions, practical skills, cultural/historical background
  • "Know what" ≠ "know how" — embodied skill knowledge cannot be encoded as bits
  • Controversial thesis that Turing's 1950 framing sent AI research in the wrong direction
