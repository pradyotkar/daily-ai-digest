🔍 Deep Dive: The Distillation Allegation

The biggest story today is the White House accusing Moonshot AI of distilling Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 to build Kimi K3 — the first time a senior US official has directly accused a specific Chinese lab of copying a specific American model.


What happened: On July 22, Michael Kratsios (OSTP Director) posted on X that Moonshot engaged in "large-scale, covert industrial distillation" and separately alleged the company obtained Nvidia GB300 chips through Thailand, bypassing export controls.


Why it matters: Kimi K3 (2.8T parameters) is the best-performing open-weight model announced this year, topping BrowseComp at 91.2%. If the distillation claim holds, it undermines the narrative of genuine Chinese AI innovation. If it doesn't, it's a diplomatic escalation based on circumstantial evidence.


The evidence: Independent analysis by Redwood Research found K3 identifies as Claude in cross-entropy tests disproportionately often. However, this could also be explained by training data contamination, leftover system prompts, or roleplay leakage. Moonshot has not conceded. No legal finding has been made.

⚡ Moonshot promised to release Kimi K3 weights by July 27 — watching that deadline is the single most important signal this week.
